Gunn Math Circle

We meet every Monday at lunch in Mr. Herreshoff’s room, N-201.

We seek to promote an appreciation and understanding of math beyond the typical high school curriculum by hosting weekly talks, in-house contests, games, and guest speakers. We also help students prepare for and participate in many contests throughout the school year.

Events & Competitions

Some of the events in which we participate as a club

AMC 10/12
Tuesday, February 3 (A) & Wednesday, February 25 (B)

Location: Gunn
The AMC 10 is a 25-question, 75-minute, multiple choice examination in secondary school mathematics containing problems which can be understood and solved with algebra and geometry concepts. The AMC 12 is a 25-question, 75-minute, multiple choice examination in secondary school mathematics containing problems which can be understood and solved with pre-calculus concepts. Two different versions of the contests are given on two dates, about two weeks apart, in February. Each AMC 10 and AMC 12 contest contain about 12 of the same math problems.
The IMO (International Mathematics Olympiad) team selection series of contests begins with the AMC's (American Mathematics Competition), which all of Gunn is eligible to take. If you're a 9th or 10th grader, you can choose between the AMC10 and the AMC12. If you're an 11th or 12th grader, you can only take the AMC12. All test versions have 25 multiple choice questions arranged in ascending order of difficulty, given 75 minutes to fill in the bubbles from A-E.

AIME - American Invitational Mathematics Examination
Thursday, March 19 (I) & Wednesday, March 25 (II)

The top AMC scorers are invited to take the AIME (American Invitational Mathematics Examination). This is a harder test where perfect scores are very rare. You are given 3 hours to solve 15 short answer questions where you have to bubble in an integer between 0 to 999, inclusive.

USA(J)MO - United States of America (Junior) Mathematical Olympiad
Tuesday & Wednesday, April 28 & 29

The top AIME scorers are invited to take the USAMO (USA Mathematical Olympiad), a 9 hour test over 2 days. You are given three questions and 4.5 hours to write up full solutions to the questions on each day.

SMT - Stanford Math Tournament
Saturday, February 14

SMT (Stanford Math Tournament) is a competition in Spring that consists 10 short answer questions. You can choose to either take two tests (choices are Algebra, Geometry, Combinatorics, Calculus) or a one general test.

BMT - Berkeley Math Tournament
Saturday, March 7

BMT (Berkeley Math Tournament) is a recently created competition which takes place in March. It's also based on HMMT.

HMMT - Harvard-MIT Mathematics Tournament
Saturday, February 21

HMMT (Harvard-MIT Mathematics Tournament) is a competition in February, which runs very similarly to SMT (because SMT is based on HMMT).

CHMMC - Caltech Harvey Mudd Math Competition
Saturday, November 22

Like the other ones.

BAMO - Bay Area Mathematical Olympiad
Late February

The BAMO (Bay Area Mathematical Olympiad) is held the last Tuesday of February. There are no prerequisites to taking this Olympiad, which consists of 5 questions in 4 hours. The difficulty range is medium AIME to easy USAMO. In addition to winning many t-shirts, Gunn typically does well enough each year to win a $150 gift card to Chevy's, which we use for our annual club dinner (at Chevy's).

ARML Power
November & January

The ARML Power round is a 60 minute team test with no cap on the maximum amount of participants. Questions range from medium AMC level to easy USAMO level.

Interstellar
First Semester

MAA-sponsored weird online competition with double elimination style tournament.

CAML - California Math League
Various Times in the Year

The CML (California Math League) is a short exam consisting of 6 questions spanning over a lunch period. This contest is the easiest it gets for competitive high school math competitions.

MathLeague
Various Times in the Year

The Mathleague series of tests is the high school version of Mathcounts, consisting of a 30 question multiple choice test (60 minutes), 10 question team round (20 minutes), and 8 question target round (10 minutes per 2 questions). The difficulty of these is around the AMC difficulty and these tests are offered about once a month.

ARML - American Regions Mathematics League
Friday & Saturday, May 29-30

In the first weekend of June, schools around the Bay Area pool together their best and brightest to represent San Francisco at the ARML competition. The "best and brightest" are selected based on USAMO/AIME scores in addition to performance on the Mathleague tests. ARML consists of an individual Target round (8 questions, 10 minutes per pair) that is in the medium to hard AIME level and two team rounds, with teams consisting of 15 people. The Power team round is a 60 minute test with proof based solutions. There is another team round with short answer solutions.

Santa Clara
November

The Santa Clara competition is a 3 hour examination with one page of questions, ranging from medium AMC level to easy AIME level. The answer format is short answer, but they use a proof based question for tie-breaking purposes.

Mad Hatter/SCVMA
March

Generally, this is an individual test of two parts. One part consists of flashing questions on an overhead projector (so once a question is deemed done by half the contestants, you can't see it again unless you jot it down). The second part is a traditional written test. The coordinators of this contest are very generous with big flashy trophies :)

USAMTS - USA Mathematical Talent Search
Six Times in the Year

USAMTS (USA Mathematical Talent Search) is a test that you take over the course of a month or so at home. You are allowed to use pretty much anything (wikipedia, wolfram alpha, an abacus, etc) except help from another person. Next round starts soon.
